Kim & Dave — the beginning
About Kim & Dave

Two lives lived generously, in the air and on the ground.

Kim and Dave met at Cal State Fullerton. Shortly after college they were married — two working professionals in sales, and aspiring balloon pilots on the side. By 1988 they had set aside their “normal” jobs in favor of moving to Temecula and becoming full-time commercial balloon pilots.

Over the next four decades they became pillars of the ballooning community — not only in Temecula, but across the entire Southwest. In great part due to Dave’s role as a DPE, and the seamless way Kim and Dave worked together on everything, when people had a question regarding aviation within the ballooning community of the Southwest, chances were one way or another those questions got escalated to Kim and Dave.

Along the way, they were entrusted with Reach for the Stars Hot Air Balloon Foundation by the founder and former director. Continuing that legacy was one of the most fulfilling parts of their ballooning journey, and the organization has grown greatly in part due to their efforts.

While their lifestyle could candidly be described as “balloonists,” they were also well known for the warmth they extended to their local community, their neighbors, and their friends. Kim was a kind soul who made friends with everyone — with a giving heart to which many would do well to aspire. Dave had a mind for teaching, a knack for meeting people where they were, and a gift for offering his opinion on a matter in a way that would be remarkably receivable to the listener.

Together they touched countless lives. It is our sincere hope that their memory can live on in our hearts — and carry forward the giving and kind demeanor we all crave to have, and to share, within our own communities.

The weekend

Three days, woven together by sky and fire.

Friday honors Dave. Saturday honors Kim. Sunday honors the pair we all knew. The memorial flight is planned for Sunday morning, where it lands on the day the two of them lived as one. Beyond that, every morning is a flight opportunity, weather permitting — we fly any chance we get. Times and launch sites will be confirmed in our WhatsApp group as the dates approach.

Weather & go / no-go

Brian Lynch will be making all weather calls for the group throughout the weekend — including go / no-go decisions and launch recommendations for both low and high flyers. Watch the WhatsApp group for his updates leading up to and during each flight window.

Friday — June 5
For Dave
  • At sunset Opening ceremony and the lighting of the fire.
  • After dark Pizza night — Dave’s favorite, and the right opener for the weekend. Brian will bring three extra-large vegan pizzas; everyone else, bring your favorite or have one delivered.
  • Around the fire Open mic — share stories of Dave, a song, a memory, or whatever the night calls for.
Saturday — June 6
For Kim
  • Throughout the day A full-spectrum day — as much fun as Kim and Dave would have if they were with us.
  • Evening Potluck dinner — Kim’s kind of gathering. Brian will make a vegan version of Kim’s family nasi goreng, a recipe passed down to her from her father. Everyone brings something to share.
  • After dinner Open mic — share stories of Kim, a song, or a memory.
Sunday — June 7
For Kim & Dave
  • Sunday morning Memorial flight — the heart of the weekend, landing on the day they lived as one. Times and launch location TBD by weather.
  • After the flight, or in lieu of it Closing ceremony — together, however the morning arrives.
  • Through the afternoon Casual hangouts and casual food. Folks head out as they’re ready, carrying the love home with them.
Coming & staying

Settle in. The week before the festival is part of the festival.

Camping on the property

  • Campers and tents are both welcome — whatever you’re comfortable in.
  • No hookups available — please come self-sufficient (water, power, waste).
  • Arrive any day during the week before. Confirm your day of arrival in the WhatsApp group.
  • Quiet hours are 10pm to 7am out of respect for everyone resting.
  • The property address is shared privately through the WhatsApp group, after your request to join is approved.

What to bring

  • Camp chairs — for around the fire and meals.
  • Firewood — the more we share, the longer the fire burns.
  • Food and drinks to share — potluck-style for both nights.
  • Layers — mornings and evenings get cool.
  • Stories about Kim & Dave — bring at least one.
We are a community on this weekend. Bring what you can, share freely, and look out for each other. That is exactly the spirit Kim and Dave lived by.
